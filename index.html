Engineering Notes

Designing for Reconnection: WebSocket State at Scale

The real design problem in WebSocket systems is not connection — it is reconnection. How to restore session state, replay missed messages, and avoid thundering herd when thousands of clients reconnect simultaneously.

In progress

The OTP Supervision Tree as an Architecture Pattern

The supervision tree is the architecture diagram. Scoping supervisors to logical failure domains keeps partial failures partial — and makes the failure model explicit rather than emergent.

In progress

Kafka vs RabbitMQ: A Systems Engineering Decision

Not a feature comparison — a decision framework. RabbitMQ models the routing problem; Kafka models the log problem. How to reason about the choice before you build.

In progress

Scaling IoT Telemetry Ingestion: Protocol Heterogeneity

MQTT, LoRaWAN, and BLE have different QoS models, payload formats, and failure characteristics. Why a generic ingestion layer is the wrong abstraction, and how protocol-specific services compose cleanly at the normalisation layer.

In progress

Backpressure in Event-Driven Systems

Bursty device telemetry will overwhelm a naive ingestion pipeline. A practical look at backpressure strategies: RabbitMQ consumer prefetch, Kafka consumer group lag, and GenStage demand-driven pipelines in Elixir.
